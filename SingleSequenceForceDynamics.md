# Single Sequence Force Dynamics

## Wiestler Transfer Experiment

Unpublished one-sequence training dataset used to study transfer between sequences.

### Training Schedule

- Day 1: Blocks 1-26
- Day 2: Blocks 27-46
- Day 3: Blocks 47-66
- Test on Day 3: Blocks 67-70

### Block Structure

- Each block contains 24 trials.
- Every 5th trial, the cue is announced and followed by five executions of the cued sequence.

### Session Phases

- Blocks 1-2: Task training on both hands; two sequences that are not repeated later.
- Blocks 3-6: Pre-test with two intrinsically connected sequence types and two control sequence types, with hand changing every block.
- Blocks 7-66: Training.
- Blocks 67-70: Post-test, structured like Blocks 3-6.

## Speed Clamping Experiment

Poster study of one common sequence trained across groups with different feedback/manipulation conditions.

### Training Schedule

- Two days of training on one sequence.
- Day 1: Blocks 1-16.
- Day 2: Blocks 17-31.

### Block Structure

- Each block contains 20 trials.
- The first block on Day 1 is a familiarization block.

### Participant Groups

- Unconstrained: normal training.
- Speed Clamped.
- Explicit + general.

### Session Phases

- The first three blocks of each day are pre-test.
- Then 8 blocks of induction.
- During induction, the speed-clamped group continues executing at the speed reached at the end of pre-test.
- The explicit + general group trains on random sequences and performs explicit recall (saying) of the original sequence at the end of each block.
- After induction, there is a post-test for 2 blocks, structured like pre-test.
- After post-test, there is a post-test clamp for all groups for 2 blocks, clamped to ET at the end of pre-test.
